Texas Attorney General Launches Investigation Into Fire Apparatus Manufacturers. Should Tennessee join?

by Hayden Bradfield

Published Friday, Mar. 13, 2026

In mid-February this year, the Texas Attorney General’s Office issued a press release with the intention of investigating fire apparatus manufacturers and their alleged involvement in illegal anticompetitive schemes. There has been an ongoing shortage of fire apparatus, ambulances, and other emergency vehicles, placing a higher burden on taxpayers and threatening public safety. In addition to the shortage of fire and emergency service personnel, an aging fleet has further compounded the already-struggling fire departments and emergency service agencies across the nation.

In a time with increased threats of terrorist sleeper cells violently lashing out, schools facing increasing threats of mass violence, and an increased prevalence of life-threatening severe weather, our emergency responders have proven to be even more valuable than ever! The motivation of many plots of terrorism is to cause as many deaths as possible. Subduing an assailant to prevent further harm is important, but aiding the wounded following an attack is just as crucial. Ensuring adequate response capabilities, including having the adequate structure, manpower, training, and the necessary life-saving equipment further prepares our state to handle disasters when they strike.

Today, I contacted the Tennessee Attorney General’s Office, urging our Attorney General to join Texas in their investigation into the shortage of emergency vehicles, to assist in determining any wrongdoing, and to take action to resolve these issues.

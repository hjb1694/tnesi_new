Individual With Prior Arrest For Arson Reportedly Serving in Captain Role At White Oak Volunteer Fire Department

by Hayden Bradfield

Published Monday, April 13, 2026

DUFF, TN — Amid mounting allegations of misappropriation of funds, leadership corruption, nepotism, and a lack of transparency at the White Oak Volunteer Fire Department, reports have been received that an individual with a previous arrest for arson and official misconduct is currently serving in a captain position at the organization. Upon conducting a brief search of court records, an individual with that name appears in the registry. Although, details are rather limited until more-thorough records can be obtained.

In 2008, this individual was arrested for arson and official misconduct while employed as a firefighter with the Jacksboro Fire Department. This person along with another firefighter reportedly started a structural fire to then be dispatched to fight it. You can view the screenshot below of the article discussing the subject.

Public-facing court records show this individual with the official misconduct charge listed with an unknown disposition.

The second screenshot shows two hearings. Oddly, the dates and times between the two hearing dates are very wide.

The third screenshot shows restitution fees being paid along with a paid expungement fee.

This individual’s name is being redacted to avoid doxxing or publicly shaming this individual. Although, this department along with its leadership should be investigated with immediate corrective action taken.

Sadly, the news media remains silent on this matter at the time of writing despite pleas to cover the story. This isn’t the first time emergency service entities in Campbell County have managed to face scrutiny. In 2025, troublesome investigation reports were released pertaining to two separate emergency service entities in the same county with findings including mismanagement of taxpayer funds and a lack of proper leadership oversight. One organization, the Stoney Fork Volunteer Fire Department, lacked a board of directors required by law and was lead by a husband/wife duo who abused funds for personal purchases.
